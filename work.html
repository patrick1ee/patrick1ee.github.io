StopDystonia App (Developer and Research Assistant)

A mobile app, based on ongoing research, designed to reduce symptoms of Dystonia (a neurological disease causing tremors and muscle spasms) via vibro-tactile stimulation using the phone's exising hardware as well as external audio devices. User-feedback and data to further the research is also collected in-app.

My role has primarily been to develop the mobile app (React Native/Expo) and a native module for utilising the phone's audio and vibration capabilities (Kotlin)", as well as the establishment of user requirements and special considerdations given the medical nature of the product and the specific target users.

EEGVIBE (Developer and Research Assistant)

A multi-threaded workflow for running closed-loop EEG experiments (in which stimulation is delivered in response to brain actiivty) and visualising and formatting the resulting data. A key challenge is being able to record activity as fast as possible whilst synchronising multiple devices (electrodes, actuators, orientation sensors etc.).

My role has been to add featrues to and improve the efficiency off the existing software, as well as refactor the codebase to ensure it is clean and self-documented such that other researchers and developers can easily build upon it.
